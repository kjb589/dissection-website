    In ECE 4724 - Embedded Systems, the dissection project allowed me to gain a deeper understanding of some of the course topics by taking apart an electronic device. I wanted to use this opportunity to learn about audio systems and how ADC/DACs work because of my interest in digital signal processing. Thus, I decided to dissect a pair of wireless active noise-cancelling headphones. This website documents each step of the dissection with a photo journal, a bill of materials for both electrical and non-electrical components, and diagrams outlining the circuitry and operation of the headphones. The links below lead to my LinkedIn, my Github, and the Amazon listing for the headphones.

    It's tough to live without a pair of noise cancelling headphones these days. For a lot of people, they are almost necessary to get through commercial flights, workouts, and public commutes. However, noise cancelling technology has been around for longer than most people realize, with the first ever pair of noise cancelling headphones being created by Bose® founder Dr. Amar Bose in 1978.
    KVIDIO's WH304B model is an affordable pair of headphones offering comfort, versatility, and disturbance-free listening. The sound quality of these headphones can not compete against more popular, expensive brands like Bose®, Apple, or Sony, but for an MSRP of just $20, they have a lot to offer.
    KVIDIO has other affordable ANC headphones option such as the WH304A, and the sister brand BERIBES offers a near identical pair in the WH305A. Both headphones can be found at Amazon, and both brands have Amazon storefronts as well.
